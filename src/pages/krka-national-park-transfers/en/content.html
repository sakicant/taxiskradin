Which Entrance Should You Use?

Krka has more than one way in, and the right one depends on where you are staying and how you want to arrive. Here is the honest version, from someone who drives it every day.

Skradin entrance. This is the one at the national park boat dock in Skradin, and for most visitors it is the best. Your park ticket includes the boat down the river to Skradinski buk, the big waterfall everyone comes to see. You arrive on the water instead of a car park, which is a far nicer way to start the day. From anywhere in Skradin the ride is a fixed €15.

Lozovac entrance. The road entrance on the other side of the falls, about 15 minutes from Skradin. From the car park a park bus takes you down to Skradinski buk. This one suits travellers coming off the motorway or anyone who would rather not wait for a boat. A fixed €25 from Skradin.

Roški Slap entrance. The quieter upper section of the park, further inland along the river. Fewer crowds, a different set of falls and the mills to walk around. Worth it if you have already seen Skradinski buk or want a calmer day. A fixed €50 from Skradin.
