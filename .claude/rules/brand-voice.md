# Brand Voice Rules

## Tone
- Sound like a knowledgeable person explaining something to a friend — clear, direct, confident
- Be concrete: name the tools, describe what actually happens, use specifics instead of abstractions
- Use action-oriented language: "connect your tools," "run this command," "type this word"
- Reassure through lowered stakes: "try it, you can't break anything," "even if you don't have these things set up yet"
- Lead with value, not features — what problem does this solve?

## Structure
- Keep sentences short — vary length for rhythm but default to concise
- Use active voice over passive voice
- Address the reader directly ("you" not "users")
- When explaining a process, follow the natural arc: what you do → what happens → what you get

## Avoid
- Buzzwords: synergy, leverage, cutting-edge, revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive, robust, innovative, seamless, utilize, delve, comprehensive, streamline, empower
- Abstract descriptions when concrete ones work: "integrates with your productivity stack" → "goes to Notion, pulls your tasks"
- Hedging: "you may wish to consider" → "do this"
- Corporate passive voice: "results were achieved" → "the team achieved X"

**Examples of violations:**
- "Our innovative solution leverages cutting-edge technology" → "We solve X by doing Y"
- "Comprehensive platform for all your needs" → "[Specific thing] for [specific audience]"
- "The system integrates with various third-party tools" → "It connects to Slack and Notion and pulls real data into your workspace"
